Hillary's Greatest Achievements:

  • After graduating from Yale Law School, Hillary had several job offers at reputable law firms. Instead, she took a job with the non-profit organization "Children's Defense Fund" dedicated to helping children.
  • While working at the "Children's Defense Fund," Hillary championed the passage of the “Individuals with Disabilities Education Act” which required states to provide quality education to all disabled children.
  • In the 1990's, First Lady Clinton fought for universal healthcare for kids. Her efforts led to “Children's Health Insurance Program” (CHIP) being passed which provided the path for an additional eight million kids to receive health coverage.
  • In the 2000's, Senator Hillary Clinton helped New York City get back on its feet after 9/11 with initiatives such as providing health coverage for Ground Zero first responders.
  • In response to the AIDS epidemic, Bill and Hillary started the Clinton Foundation which has provided HIV medicine to over ten million HIV-infected Africans.
  • From 2009 to 2013, Secretary Clinton visited 112 countries and brokered for peace and cooperation so that the United States maintained its position as the world's leading superpower.
  • As Secretary of State, Hillary was heavily involved in the raid that succeeded in killing the architect of 9/11, Osama Bin Laden.
  • In 2016, ninety-six years after women received the right to vote, Hillary Clinton became the first woman in U.S. history to receive a presidential nomination from a major political party.
  • On November 8th, 2016 Hillary Clinton received over 65.8 million votes for president of the United States. Despite having nearly a three million vote lead in the popular vote, Hillary narrowly lost the Electoral College by about 100,000 total votes in a few swing states.
